The steps below are needed to verify the code in the calvin/scripts/test1.calvin example:

generate private key and x509 certificate:
openssl req -x509 -newkey rsa:2048 -keyout ~/eric-calvin/privkey.pem -out ~/eric-calvin/cert.pem

extract pulic key (only needed for cmd-line verification of signatures):
openssl rsa -in ~/eric-calvin/privkey.pem -pubout > ~/eric-calvin/pubkey.pem

sign actor:
openssl dgst -sha256 -sign ~/eric-calvin/privkey.pem -out ~/eric-calvin/calvin/actorstore/systemactors/std/CountTimer.sign ~/eric-calvin/calvin/actorstore/systemactors/std/CountTimer.py
openssl dgst -sha256 -sign ~/eric-calvin/privkey.pem -out ~/eric-calvin/calvin/actorstore/systemactors/io/StandardOut.sign ~/eric-calvin/calvin/actorstore/systemactors/io/StandardOut.py

sign calvin script:
openssl dgst -sha256 -sign ~/eric-calvin/privkey.pem -out ~/eric-calvin/calvin/scripts/test1.sign ~/eric-calvin/calvin/scripts/test1.calvin

(Not really needed) Example procedure of how to verify a signature in openssl:
openssl dgst -sha256 -verify ~/eric-calvin/pubkey.pem  -signature ~/eric-calvin/calvin/scripts/test1.sign ~/eric-calvin/calvin/scripts/test1.calvin
